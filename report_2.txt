**Patient History:**
- Age: 2 years old
- Family History: Non-contributory
- Medical History: No significant medical history
- Allergies: No known allergies

**Clinical Findings:**
- High fever for 5 days
- Irritability
- Conjunctivitis
- Strawberry tongue
- Inflammation of the hands and feet
- Desquamation of the skin of the fingers and toes
- Cervical lymphadenopathy (smallest node 1.5 cm)
- Abdominal tenderness and enlarged liver
- Elevated alanine aminotransferase
- White blood cell count of 17,580/mm
- Albumin 2.1 g/dL
- C-reactive protein 4.5 mg
- Erythrocyte sedimentation rate 60 mm/h
- Mild normochromic, normocytic anemia
- Leukocytes in urine of 20/mL with no bacteria identified
- Moderate dilation of the coronary arteries with possible coronary artery aneurysm

**Diagnosis:**
Kawasaki Disease

**Treatment Options:**
1. Intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG) therapy: 2 g/kg as a single infusion over 10-12 hours, ideally within the first 10 days of illness.
2. High-dose aspirin therapy: 80-100 mg/kg/day in 4 divided doses until the patient is afebrile for at least 48 hours, then 3-5 mg/kg/day for a total of 6-8 weeks.
3. If the patient does not respond to IVIG, a second dose of IVIG may be considered, along with additional therapies such as corticosteroids or infliximab.
